Static versus Dynamic Websites

Static websites appear the same for every user and only change when the source files are modified, whereas dynamic websites can present different information to different visitors.


Static

Most portfolio websites are static. The owner picks and chooses what projects they want to display for employers. I mostly like the color pallet and layout of the website. I also really like how his personality shows through in the design, with handwritten fonts placed around. I don't really like how so much of the work is just grouped in the bottom, I don't think that that's what people like to see on landing pages. It also links to a dynamic website, Etsy, where the artist sells his work.

Dynamic

Netflix shows a unique catalogue to each of its user and the layout of endless scrolling and swiping is copied by many other streaming services, meaning Netflix is the blueprint. I also spend a lot of time there so I appreciate that it is personalized. I do dislike how it sometimes seems like you can't find anything good to watch but I am unsure of how that problem could be remedied.


Not sure where this site falls under but it is very entertaining. Peter Oravec - Portfolio
